The Canadian Museum for Human Rights (CMHR; French: Mus√©e canadien pour les droits de la personne) is a Crown company and national museum located in Winnipeg, Manitoba, near The Forks. The museum's mission is to "examine the subject of human rights with a specific but not exclusive focus on Canada, to improve the public's awareness of human rights, to promote respect for others, and to inspire contemplation and conversation."


The CMHR was established in 2008 as a result of the passage of Bill C-42, which amended the Museums Act of Canada. It is Canada's first new national museum since 1967, and it is also the country's first national museum to be situated outside of the National Capital Region. On September 19, 2014, the Museum performed its opening ceremony.
