The Canadian government's strong commitments towards religious diversity and Multicultural Accommodation.

The multicultural policy reflects the Canadian government's strong commitments towards religious diversity and Multicultural Accommodation. According to a Pew Research Center research based on information from 2016, the most current year available, Canada has minimal levels of government restrictions on religion . Canada's constitution protects the freedom of religion by providing Multicultural Accommodation. For example, in 2006, the Supreme Court ruled that a Quebec school could not restrict a Sikh student from carrying a symbolic kirpan (dagger) at school, which Sikhs are obliged to take as part of their faith . Sikh men in the Royal Canadian Mounted Police are likewise exempted from wearing hats, allowing him to wear turbans as their faith requires . Most recently in the year 2018, a Quebec court ruled that a Muslim woman was incorrectly forced to remove her hijab in the courtroom and that headscarves should be permitted in court provided they do not harm the public interest . I strongly believe that the modifications to the regulations reflect this government's strong commitment to multicultural society by allowing believers the freedom to assemble and worship without limitation or interference.

The first Mountie to wear a turban, Baltej Dhillon,

is retiring from the RCMP after nearly 30 years with the force. Dhillon formally applied to the force in 1988 and passed all the entrance requirements. But at the time, the RCMP dress code banned both turbans and beards — key components of his Sikh faith.



Bill 21

Quebec's Superior Court is upholding most of the province's secularism law, known as Bill 21, which includes a ban on religious symbols worn by government workers.
